SAT

SAT(Scholastic Assessment Test) is a Standardised Test accepted in Universities of USA, UK, Canada Singapore, Germany, Australia, etc. It allows the Universities to understand the ability of the student in Critical Reading, Writing, and Math. It is administered by CollegeBoard. It is taken by students to prove their ability to take admissions in Undergraduate programs in Universities in the countries mentioned above. It was a paper-based standardized test till 2022 but from 2023, it is going to be completely Digital for international students.


SAT is available all over the world except in some critical places. Digital SAT will be conducted 7 times annually instead of 5 as previously for all international applicants.


The Digital SAT will be conducted on an application called ‘BlueBook’, on which you can take some mock tests of The Digital SAT.


However, the test is now completely digital, but still the test will be conducted at the Designated Test Centers only.


You can bring your own device (Laptop or Tablet) to the test center, but if you don’t have one, you can request the College Board to provide a device at the test center.


● Score Range- 400(min) - 1600(max)


● Duration of the test- 134 minutes


● Testing Skills- Critical Reading, Writing, and Math


● Eligibility- Not mentioned but  English Fluency is Assumed.


● Website- https://satsuite.collegeboard.org


● Age Criteria- None but 14+ is recommended



Pattern-



Introduction :-


The Digital SAT is now based on Multistage Adaptive Testing, which means if you perform better, the questions would become harder, and if you perform a bit badly, then the questions would become easier.


In the Digital SAT, the reading passages became shorter with a single question, unlike the Paper-Based SAT, where 10-12 questions were asked from one single passage. Now, the Reading Section and the writing section are submerged into one section called the ‘Reading and Writing Section’. The question types of the Reading and Writing section are divided into Craft & Structure, Information & Ideas, Standard English Conventions, and Expression of Ideas.


Now you can use the calculator for the whole Math section. The Math section is further divided into Algebra, Advanced Math, Problem-solving & Data Analysis, and Trigonometry & Geometry.


You get an On-Screen Graphing Calculator during the test, but you can bring your own as well.



Duration :-


The Digital SAT is shorter than the Paper-Based SAT. It is now only 2h 14min.


The Reading and Writing section has 64 minutes to solve 54 questions. The Reading and Writing section are divided into two modules of 32 minutes consisting of 27 questions each.


The Math section has 70 minutes to solve 44 questions. The math section is also divided into two modules of 35 minutes containing 22 questions each.



Things better in Digital SAT:


1.  If the battery of the device dies, then you can plug your device back without losing your progress and time.


2.   You get a timer on the screen, but if you find it disturbing, then you can hide it.


3.   You get a built-in Graphing Calculator on the screen.


4.   You can Flag the question which you want to attempt later.


5.   A list of formulas will be available for reference in the Math section during the test .


6.   You can highlight the question by taking note of what you want to highlight.


7.   Now you will get the result in the matter of days instead of weeks.

Preparation Tips and Material :-

Reading- Start by reading about a wide range of science and humanities subjects. Begin with the New York Times, and Washington Post. You should immediately begin reading the editorials. Discover current environmental and geopolitical issues. Work on becoming a more knowledgeable reader as a whole. Try developing your own opinions as opposed to passively absorbing information.


Writing and Language- Know the Advanced Grammar Rules and know punctuation really well. Develop the Vocabulary.


Math- Start putting all of the math concepts covered by the current SAT into practice. Additionally, the official ACT geometry and trigonometry questions merit special attention. Facts and formulas are needed for math. They help you respond to questions more effectively and accurately. Therefore, exert yourself to master the formulas and facts associated with the current SAT topics while also comprehending their justification and operation.


You can refer Khan Academy SAT Blackbook, BlueBook Application, The Princeton Review SAT, Barron’s book, and The Official SAT Guide for better marks.
