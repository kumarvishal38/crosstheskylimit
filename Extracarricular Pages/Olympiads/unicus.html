What is Unicus Olympiads

Unicus Olympiads are exams designed to foster a student's foundation and increase their curiosity. They aim to provide a platform for students to have a clear understanding of previous concepts, dig deeper into their studies, and prepare for future exams. Unicus Olympiads also encourage a pragmatic approach to problem-solving. They are held in the summer to avoid conflicting with the school curriculum. The name "Unicus" is Latin for "unique," and the goal of these Olympiads is to help students stand out and develop a competitive spirit.


1.The Unicus Science Olympiad (USO) is an exam that tests students' knowledge of science. It is held online and in person for students in grades 1 to 11. Students can take three mock tests to practice before the final exam, which is a multiple-choice test. The exam has two sections: the Classic Section, which covers practical applications of science, and the Scholar Section, which focuses on higher-order thinking skills. The syllabus for the USO covers the topics studied in the previous two years for grades 3 to 11, and covers environmental science for grade 2, and topics taught in pre-primary classes for grade 1. Students in grades 2 to 11 have 60 minutes to complete the exam, while students in grade 1 have 45 minutes. Eligibility - class 1st to 11th More Details - https://www.unicusolympiads.com/uso


2.The Unicus Mathematics Olympiad (UMO) is an exam that tests students' knowledge of math. It is held online and in person for students in grades 1 to 11. Students can take three mock tests to practice before the final exam, which is a multiple-choice test. The exam has two sections: the Classic Section, which covers practical applications of math, and the Scholar Section, which focuses on higher-order thinking skills. The syllabus for the UMO covers the topics studied in the previous two years for grades 3 to 11 and covers topics taught in pre-primary classes for grade 1. Students in grades 2 to 11 have 60 minutes to complete the exam, while students in grade 1 have 45 minutes. Eligibility - Class 1st to 11th More Details - https://www.unicusolympiads.com/umo


3. The Unicus English Olympiad (UEO) is an exam that tests students' knowledge of the English language. It is held online and in person for students in grades 1 to 11. Students can take three mock tests to practice before the final exam, which is a multiple-choice test. The exam has two sections: the Classic Section, which covers practical concepts of the English language, and the Scholar Section, which focuses on higher-order thinking skills. The syllabus for the UEO covers the topics studied in the previous two years for grades 3 to 11 and covers topics taught in pre-primary classes for grade 1. Students in grades 2 to 11 have 60 minutes to complete the exam, while students in grade 1 have 45 minutes. Eligibility - class 1st to 11th More Details - https://www.unicusolympiads.com/ueo


4.The Unicus General Knowledge Olympiad (UGKO) is an exam that tests students' knowledge of general knowledge and current affairs. It is held online and in person for students in grades 2 to 11. Students can take three mock tests to practice before the final exam, which is a multiple-choice test that must be completed in 60 minutes. The UGKO covers the topics studied in the previous two years for grades 3 to 11 and covers topics studied in grades 1 for grade 2. The exam has two sections: the Classic Section, which covers general knowledge, and the Scholar Section, which focuses on current affairs. Eligibility - Class 2nd to 11th More Details - https://www.unicusolympiads.com/ugko


5.The Unicus Critical Thinking Olympiad (UCTO) is a competitive exam for students in classes 2-11. It is held online and offline and includes three mock tests (including a free trial) to help students prepare for the final exam. The final exam is a multiple-choice test that students must complete in 60 minutes. The syllabus for classes 2-11 covers topics from the previous two classes, while the syllabus for class 2 covers topics from class 1. The exam consists of two sections: the Classic Section, which includes questions on logical reasoning, and the Scholar Section, which includes questions on advanced thinking skills in reasoning. Eligibility - class 2nd to 11th More Details - https://www.unicusolympiads.com/ucto


6.The Unicus Non-Routine Mathematics Olympiad (UNRMO) is an international math competition held online for students in grades 3-10. The exam tests students' problem-solving skills with non-traditional math questions, preparing them for real-world problem-solving. Students can take three practice tests before the final exam, which is a multiple-choice test with a 60-minute time limit. There are two levels to the exam: Level 1 is free and simpler, while Level 2 is more challenging and requires a fee of $20 for international students or INR 300 for Indian students. Level 2 also offers merit certificates, medals, and trophies. The exam includes a Classic section with non-routine math questions and a Scholar section with higher-order thinking skills questions. Eligibility - Class 3rd to 10th More Details - https://www.unicusolympiads.com/unrmo
